Customer Segmentation by Spending Level
Project Overview

This project focuses on segmenting customers based on their spending behavior. The goal is to identify high-value customers, understand their spending patterns, and create actionable insights that can support targeted marketing, loyalty programs, or business strategy decisions.

Objective
Segment customers into different groups based on their total spending.
Identify the top spenders for focused business attention.
Use SQL analytical functions to achieve precise segmentation.

Methodology

Data Analysis

Used the mall.Customers to extract customer transactions, including purchase amounts.
Ensured data was clean and suitable for segmentation (handling missing or inconsistent values).

Segmentation Approach

Applied CASE WHEN statements to classify customers into spending categories (e.g., low, medium, high).
Used the NTILE() function to divide customers into quantiles, allowing identification of the top spenders.

Analysis

Segmented customers into spending levels to find who contributes most to revenue.
Highlighted the high-value customers for targeted business strategies.
Visualized segmentation with charts (PNG images provided in the repository) to better understand spending distribution.

Tools and Skills Applied

SQL Functions: CASE WHEN, NTILE(), SUM(), GROUP BY, ORDER BY
Data Analysis: Customer-level aggregation and ranking
Visualization: PNG charts to illustrate top spenders and spending distribution
Analytical Thinking: Identification of actionable business insights from spending patterns

Outcome

Clear segmentation of customers into Low, Medium, High spenders.
Identification of the top-tier customers driving maximum revenue.
Insights that can be very useful in or marketing campaigns, loyalty rewards, and strategic business decisions.
